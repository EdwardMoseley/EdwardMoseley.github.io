(Preliminary) Project Description

This Master's Capstone is an investigation into various Bioinformatics/Computational Biology tools used for the prediction of protein-protein/protein-small molecule interactions, and/or the prediction of Gene Ontologies (GO) on the basis of protein sequence data, genome wide association studies (GWAS), or other data sources.

The project will begin fairly broad in scope, but is expected to narrow significantly through meetings with professors and possible collaborators. If a project with privately-generated data is not available, public data sources will be employed.

On Tuesday, June 13th, I met with a number of individuals from the School of Veterinary Medicine, as well as external collaborators from MIT and industry.

The general project relates to the study of "VHHs", which are the variable domains of heavy-chain antivodies (HCAb), which are specific to the Camelid biological family. The overview of the biological experimental design was reviewed in detail, and includes:

(Animal) Study Design

  1. Pre-Immunization blood-draw, Peripheral Blood Lymphocyte (PBL) isolation, DNA sequencing to determine Pre-Immunization VHH repertoir.
  2. Prime/Boost regimen, where blood draws are performed immediately prior to immunization and 2-weeks post-vaccination for PBL isolation and VHH sequencing.

Bioinformatics Study Design

For the bioinformatics analysis, we still have some details to iron out. However, the overall objective appears to relate to tracking the evolution of these cells through the process of somatic hypermutation and clonal selection.

It is unclear, at this point, if we will attempt to model the interaction between the antibodies and the antigenic material the animals were immunized against. However, it would be very interesting to see if we could use the VHH amino acid sequence data to predict the avidity (per protein-capture ELISA titers, or SPR data via Biacore) with which the VHHs would bind to the antigenic material they received during immunization.
